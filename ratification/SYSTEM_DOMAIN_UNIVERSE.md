## II. Core Civilization Systems (Foundational Platforms)

### 1. Digital Identity & Trust Platform

**Definition:**  
A coherent system assembling identity, access, trust, and compliance capabilities into a unified platform for establishing and verifying digital identity and trust relationships across organizational boundaries.

**Core Organs Composed:**
- Identity & Access Management
- Trust & Compliance
- Governance & Policy

**Scope:**  
Comprehensive identity lifecycle management, federated authentication, authorization policy enforcement, trust establishment and verification, compliance attestation, cross-organizational identity federation, and trust relationship management.

**Exclusions:**  
Does not include payment identity verification, marketplace reputation systems, workforce-specific identity management, or customer relationship identity tracking.

**Stability Rationale:**  
Digital identity and trust are foundational to all digital interactions. This system represents a stable platform capability that transcends specific organizational contexts and remains architecturally coherent across all deployment scenarios.

## III. Extensible Systems

Systems may be introduced beyond the Core Civilization Systems.

**Requirements for Extensible Systems:**

- Must represent coherent domain
- Must assemble existing Organs
- Must pass structural audit
- Must not merge unrelated capability domains
- Must not duplicate existing Systems

## IV. Structural Rules

The following structural rules are locked and immutable:

- Systems must remain coherent
- Systems cannot redefine Organ boundaries
- Systems cannot collapse into monoliths
- Systems cannot embed deployment logic
- Systems cannot bypass Tissue abstraction

## V. Amendment Rule

Modifications to the System Domain Universe are governed by formal procedures:

**Adding New System:**  
Requires domain coherence validation, Organ composition verification, structural audit, and Founder ratification.

**Splitting a System:**  
Requires domain boundary analysis, coherence validation for resulting Systems, structural audit, and Founder ratification.

**Merging Systems:**  
Requires justification for domain unification, coherence validation for merged System, structural audit, and Founder ratification.
